Our cycling trip through Slovenia - from Prague by train to Graz, then six days exploring Slovenia's diverse landscapes by bike.

We took the morning train from Prague to Graz, arriving before noon, ready for six days of cycling through Slovenia. The plan was simple: explore as much of this small country as possible, then catch the late afternoon train back to Prague.

Day 1: Graz to Žalec

Graz to Žalec 159.8 km 1,337 m gain

Started cycling south from Graz train station around noon. Crossed the Austrian-Slovenian border on quiet country roads - just a simple sign marking the change.

The landscape shifted from Austrian rolling hills to Slovenian countryside with traditional red-tiled roof villages. Roads were surprisingly quiet and well-maintained.

Žalec sits in Slovenia's hop-growing region - we could smell the hops in the air. The town has the world's first beer fountain, which seemed like the right way to end day one after 160km.

Day 2: Žalec to Gorizia

Žalec to Gorizia 194.3 km 1,427 m gain

Our longest day - nearly 200km crossing Slovenia west to the Italian border. Started early through the Savinja Valley region.

The route took us through traditional Slovenian villages, each with church spires and scattered farmhouses. Roads stayed quiet throughout. By afternoon, the landscape changed as we approached the Italian border - more vineyards, Mediterranean influences in the architecture.

Gorizia is split between Italy (Gorizia) and Slovenia (Nova Gorica). After 194km, Italian food was exactly what we needed.

Day 3: Gorizia to Bohinj

Gorizia to Bohinj 159.1 km 1,898 m gain

The climbing day - 1,898m elevation gain heading into the Julian Alps. Left Italy early morning, immediately started climbing into proper mountain territory.

Followed roads alongside the Soča River - the water has this incredible emerald green color that's completely unique. The Julian Alps, dominated by Mount Triglav (2,864m - Slovenia's highest peak), provided the backdrop.

Lake Bohinj sits in Triglav National Park, Slovenia's only national park. This glacial lake is much wilder than touristy Lake Bled - surrounded by peaks and pristine forest.

Day 4: Bohinj to Slovenj Gradec

Bohinj to Slovenj Gradec

Left the high Alps behind, descending through valleys that showcase Slovenia's biodiversity. The country packs incredible landscape variety into just 20,273 square kilometers.

Slovenj Gradec is a well-preserved medieval town with Gothic architecture. Much quieter than Slovenia's bigger cities.

Day 5: Slovenj Gradec to Maribor

Slovenj Gradec to Maribor

Rode through Slovenia's wine-growing region toward Maribor, the country's second-largest city. The terrain was gentler - rolling hills covered in vineyards.

Maribor sits on the Drava River near the Austrian border. The city is home to the world's oldest vine - over 400 years old and still producing grapes.

Day 6: Maribor to Graz (by train)

Maribor to Graz (by train)

Took the train back to Graz instead of cycling. Arrived with time to catch the afternoon train to Prague, departing before 17:00.
